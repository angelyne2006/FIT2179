When Rain Becomes a Disaster

Understanding Malaysia's Flood Crisis

Malaysia floods every year — but how bad is it, and who bears the cost?

Intro

Introduction

Every year, Malaysia's Northeast Monsoon brings torrential rain to the East Coast — and with it, floods that displace thousands, destroy homes, and cost the nation hundreds of millions of ringgit. This visualisation traces the 2024–2025 flood season: where the rain fell hardest, who was displaced, and what it cost.

01

Malaysia's Monsoon: When and Where Rain Becomes a Threat

Malaysia's floods are driven by the Northeast Monsoon. It brings heavy rainfall to East Coast states between November and March.

Average monthly rainfall peaks during the Northeast Monsoon (November to March), with East Coast states receiving the heaviest rainfall.

Kelantan and Terengganu record the highest rainfall across all months of 2025.

Several East Coast states recorded rainfall far above historical averages in 2025, signalling an anomalous wet season.

02

When Floods Hit: The Human and Environmental Impact

When the rains come, consequences extend beyond water levels — homes are destroyed, people displaced, and the land itself suffers.

Terengganu and Kelantan recorded the highest flood losses in 2025, reflecting their exposure to the Northeast Monsoon.

Flood-hit states show lower vegetation health in early 2025, with Johor, Sabah and Sarawak recording the most evacuees.

Evacuations spiked repeatedly from January to May 2025. Note: 143,927 more evacuees were recorded across multiple states in December 2024.

Infrastructure losses rose in 2025 even as total losses fell, suggesting floods cause deeper structural damage over time.

03

The Price Malaysia Pays: Economic Damage

Floods cost Malaysia hundreds of millions of ringgit every year — but the burden is not shared equally across states.

2021 saw catastrophic losses of RM6.1 billion following Tropical Depression 29W. Losses have since stabilised at RM600–900 million annually.

Smaller states like Perlis and Terengganu bear the heaviest burden per person, despite lower total losses than larger states.

Terengganu and Kelantan recorded the highest total flood losses in 2025, driven by the Northeast Monsoon.
